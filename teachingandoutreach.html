Teaching and Outreach

An underlying theme across my work in teaching and outreach is my belief in the power of curiousity, critical thinking, an open mind, and a seed of knowledge. In short, my teaching philosphy is centered around experiential, student-driven learning. My goal is that, when I communicate science, people leave our interaction with an improved understanding of the natural world and a renewed sense of their own deductive reasoning. Not everyone will seek a career in geology, but in teaching the geosciences, we can empower people to ask hard questions, make observations, and form their own interpretations about the world they live in. These skills are foundational to science and society alike.

University-level teaching

I have had the distinct pleasure of teaching Geosci627: Hydrogeology (in fall 2021 and upcoming fall 2023) and Geosci320: Geomorphology (in spring 2022) at the University of Wisconsin-Madison as a Graduate Teaching Assistant.

K-12 teaching

I was summer faculty with GeoFORCE Texas from 2020-2023. GeoFORCE is an education and outreach program that aims to increase underrepresented minority participation in earth sciences, in part by giving high school students field- and research-based learning experiences in a 4-year progression of summer academies. I taught and developed content for 10th grade and 12th grade academies which focus on sedimentology, stratigraphy, landscape evolution, and planetary geomorphology and habitability. I believe strongly in GeoFORCE's mission to diversify geoscience by empowering underrepresented students, and I love getting to play my part.
